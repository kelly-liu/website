PROJECTS

ScriptSpeak

accessible communication for everyone

Problem

How can we help those with speaking disabilities communicate with others in all environments?

Solution

A user-customizable iOS app in which users can input, play, and organize their own phrases so that they can communicate more quickly. The app is more affordable, faster, and easier to use than any existing assistive technology for this purpose.

Lessons Learned

A client's actions are often more revealing than their words. A user does not always know what their biggest problem is or how to fix it. But by observing their actions, we can maybe find the answer.

Handwritten Music Reader

digitizing handwritten musical scores

Problem

How can we help musicians create digital compositions in a more intuitive manner?

Solution

An application that takes in a picture of a handwritten musical score, processes it (using methods from OpenCV), and outputs a corresponding digital data model. With this, musicians do not need to learn any new software to create digital compositions.

Lessons Learned

We often take for granted the ability for humans to accurately interpret all sorts of data. For computer vision projects, we must clearly constrain the input data and broaden it only once we've solved the constrained problem.

MITxplore

an online hub for events at MIT

Problem

How can we help students find the events they care about at MIT?

Solution

An application that centralizes all event information at MIT. Event information is extracted from popular event list pages or input by students. Through the app, students can 1) view all events on campus 2) find interesting events to attend and 3) publicize their own.

Lessons Learned

It is so important to have a realistic minimum viable product for our first prototype. It helps us understand the essence of our project and provides better direction for future improvements.

PetalPlayer

personalized online educational video learning

Problem

How can we help people pay better attention and learn more during online educational videos?

Solution

An Android application that detects user attentiveness levels and starts/stops a video accordingly to help users focus more during videos.The application uses OpenCV methods to detect various forms of inattentiveness, such as sleepiness, spacing out, and distractedness.

Lessons Learned

When learning a new technology, google google google until it works. Also, creating clear + tangible timelines is a thing too.

Design for America

a community designing for social impact

Problem

How can we encourage MIT students to build confidence in their technical skills and pursue projects that make a difference?

Solution

Creating an organization that helps student teams social impact through engineering and design. DFA provides mentorship, technical support, and a supportive community for students to effectively design solutions to real world problems.

Lessons Learned

Bridging the Gap

a lightweight mobile approach to learning physics

Problem

How can we help incoming university students in South Africa succeed in first year physics and stay in school?

Solution

An lightweight, feature phone friendly, online bridge course that teaches the conceptual understanding and rigorous problem solving skills needed for university level courses. This course is an easily accessible resource for anyone with limited internet, thus making it a solid supplement for the rote learning done in many secondary schools.

Lessons Learned

To working on projects with multiple stakeholders, you must hold fast to your goals, be patient, make sense of ambiguity, and have a whole lot of creativity.

Lighting in Uganda

hands-on education about low-cost lighting

Problem

How can we help students in refugee settlements learn the STEM concepts needed to make their own practical lighting sources?

Solution

A self-guided booklet that students can follow to build a low-cost, sustainable, bright light for reading and lighting rooms. The curriculum will be highly visual + interactive. The lighting project will be designed with ease of assembly in mind.

Lessons Learned

International development projects require the designer to adapt to a completely unfamiliar set of constraints, but it also helps you realize that you can still accomplish a lot with what you once thought was very little.

Computer Science and You

empowering students through computer science

Problem

How can we increase interest in computer science amongst middle schoolers?

Solution

A curriculum that explores how we can use computers to express, text, or accomplish our ideas. The curriculum will humanize computers by highlighting the strengths and weaknesses of computers and humans, and then focusing on how we can communicate with computers to manipulate those strengths.

Lessons Learned

Creating good curriculum requires a deep understanding of what drives a student's interest. Middle schoolers need content that relates to their identity growth and self-expression.

Society of Women Engineers

getting kids excited about STEM

Problem

How can we make STEM more approachable to kids of all ages?

Solution

For high school students Organize College Connection, a worldwide e-mentorship program that connects aspiring high school girls to MIT female students who advise them through college applications and career planning

For elementary school studentsStart Science Saturdays, a program that makes science applicable and fun using hands-on experiments that link to real-world situations

Lessons Learned

Students interested in STEM tend to be a self selected group. You must go to the right locations, be consistent, and be persistent to really impact others.

ML + Human-Robot Interactions

using RFID tags for robot prototyping

Problem

How can we make robot sensor prototyping quicker, cheaper, and easier for the general public?

Solution

Use RFID tags as low-latency, low-power sensors that detect touch, motion, and proximity of the robot to surrounding objects through Monte Carlo methods.
